When Your IRP Renewal Stalls — And Your Job Hangs in the Balance

Piotr's story from Limerick | Immigration and Residence Permits

Piotr had been working in Limerick for three years on a valid Irish Residence Permit. He'd built a stable life here—a job with a good employer, familiar routines, and plans for the future. When his IRP came due for renewal, he did everything right. He submitted his application well in advance, paid the fee, and waited for the new card to arrive. But months passed. Then six months. The application seemed to vanish into silence.

The real shock came when his employer called him in. They'd noticed his IRP documentation had expired and made it clear that working without valid immigration status wasn't an option. Even though Piotr had applied for renewal, the fact that he had no physical proof troubled them. His employer gave him a deadline: get current documentation or face termination. Piotr felt trapped between a system that wouldn't process his renewal and an employer who couldn't wait any longer.

What Piotr needed was legal proof that his application was genuine and pending—something concrete to show his employer while he pursued answers from immigration officials. A solicitor obtained written confirmation from the immigration authorities acknowledging that his IRP renewal application was in the system and under review. Armed with this letter, Piotr was able to show his employer that he was doing everything required of him. The letter satisfied his employer's concerns about employing someone without valid documentation, buying him the time he needed while the renewal eventually went through.

What the Law Says

Under Irish immigration law, when you apply for renewal of your IRP, you have the legal right to work while your application is being processed, provided you applied before your current permit expired or within a reasonable timeframe. However, employers are entitled to see valid documentation of your status. If your physical card is delayed, written confirmation from the immigration service that your renewal is pending provides legal protection for both you and your employer. This confirmation demonstrates that you have complied with the renewal process and are not working unlawfully.

Important Time Limits

⏱ Act Before Your Status Lapses

Do not wait for delays to resolve on their own. These situations can escalate quickly:

  • Apply for IRP renewal before your current card expires — never after, as this creates a legal gap
  • If your renewal is delayed beyond expected timeframes, seek legal advice within 4–6 weeks of the delay becoming apparent
  • Obtain written confirmation of your pending application as soon as delays occur — don't wait until your employer raises concerns
  • Keep all application receipts, emails, and correspondence in one place

Delays in IRP renewals can affect your employment, access to services, and travel. The sooner you get legal confirmation of your pending status, the sooner you can protect your position.
