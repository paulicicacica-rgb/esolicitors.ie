Join Family Visa Refused – Appeal Success

Miroslava, living in Dublin, applied for a join family visa to bring her spouse from abroad to Ireland. The application seemed straightforward — she had the income, stable employment, and a genuine marriage. When the refusal letter arrived from the Immigration, Naturalisation and Residence Office (INRO), it was a shock. The officer had questioned whether the marriage itself was genuine, citing concerns about the timeline and nature of the relationship. Miroslava felt her personal life was being interrogated without fair hearing.

Rather than accept the decision, Miroslava decided to appeal. She gathered everything that told the real story of her relationship — years of photographs, messages, holiday bookings, witness statements from friends who knew them both, bank statements showing shared finances, and even a detailed written account of how they met and built their life together. She wanted to show that this wasn't a paper marriage or a scheme; this was two people in love, wanting to build a life in Ireland together.

The appeal submission was thorough and human. Instead of just countering the legal points, it painted the full picture of the relationship. The weight of genuine evidence — the lived reality documented over time — spoke louder than any doubt the original officer had raised. When the appeal decision came back, the visa was granted. The relationship was real, and it had always been real. Miroslava could finally bring her spouse home.

What the Law Says

Under Irish immigration law, applicants for a join family visa must prove genuine family relationships and meet specific financial and housing requirements. When INRO refuses an application on the grounds of authenticity, applicants have the right to appeal the decision to the International Protection Appeals Tribunal or through administrative review, depending on the visa type. The burden is on the applicant to provide evidence, but the decision-maker must apply proper legal standards and fair procedure. Genuine evidence of relationship history — including communication, shared life, and corroboration — is central to overturning authenticity concerns.

Important: Time Limits

Appeals of visa refusal decisions must typically be submitted within a strict timeframe (usually 30 calendar days from the refusal letter). Missing this deadline can mean losing your right to appeal altogether. If your join family visa has been refused, act quickly and seek advice immediately to understand your options and protect your rights.
