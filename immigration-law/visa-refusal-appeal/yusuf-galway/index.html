Yusuf's Work Permit: From Refusal to Success

A documentation error turned around on appeal

Yusuf had secured a job offer from a Galway tech company and was excited to start his new role. He'd found a company willing to sponsor his work permit, and all the pieces seemed to be in place. But when his application came back, it was rejected. The Department had identified what they called a "technical deficiency" in the employer's documentation. Reading the refusal letter, Yusuf felt the ground shift beneath him. The job offer was still valid, but without a work permit, he couldn't take it.

The refusal didn't make sense to Yusuf at first. His employer had filled out all the forms, provided the required business records, and submitted everything on time. Yet the Department said one of the key documents was incomplete—a form that proved the employer had the capacity to hire and pay him. Looking back at what had been sent, they found it: a small but crucial section of the employer's payroll declaration had been left blank. It was an honest mistake, but in immigration law, even small gaps can mean a file gets rejected.

Rather than start from scratch with a new application, Yusuf decided to appeal the refusal. He worked with his employer to complete the missing information correctly this time, gathered supporting evidence to show the company was genuinely solvent and capable of employing him, and submitted everything as part of his appeal. The Department reviewed the corrected documentation, acknowledged the error had been corrected, and overturned the original refusal. Within weeks, Yusuf had his work permit in hand and was able to start his job.

What the Law Says

Work permit applications must meet strict documentary requirements set by the Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment. If an application is refused because required documents are incomplete or contain errors, you have the right to appeal the decision. An appeal allows you to submit corrected or additional documentation to address the Department's concerns. The Department will review the appeal and may overturn the original refusal if the deficiency has been genuinely remedied. Appeals must be made within specific timeframes and should clearly explain how you've addressed each point raised in the refusal letter.

⏱ Important Time Limits

You must submit an appeal of a work permit refusal within 4 weeks of receiving the refusal letter. This deadline is strict and extensions are rarely granted. If you miss it, you'll need to submit a new application from the start. Make sure you keep the original refusal letter safe and note the date you received it. When you appeal, be clear about what documents you're submitting to correct the original problem.
