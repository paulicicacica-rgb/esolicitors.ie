Do you need a solicitor for the WRC?

You are not legally required to have a solicitor at a Workplace Relations Commission hearing — you can represent yourself. However, employers almost always have legal representation. Going in without a solicitor significantly reduces your chances of success, particularly in unfair dismissal cases where the legal tests are precise.

Conditional fee arrangements

Some employment solicitors in Ireland will take on strong cases on a conditional fee basis — meaning their full fee is only payable if you win. Others charge a reduced fee upfront with a success element. This is worth asking about directly, particularly if the value of your claim is significant.

Maximum WRC awards

The WRC can award up to two years' salary for unfair dismissal. For a person earning €40,000 per year, that is a potential award of €80,000. Against that backdrop, spending €2,000–€3,000 on proper legal representation is a sound investment.

Settlement before hearing

Most employment disputes settle before the WRC hearing. An experienced employment solicitor will often resolve your case through negotiation — saving you the stress of a formal hearing and potentially recovering your costs from the employer as part of the settlement.
