Personal Injury Solicitor
Costs in Ireland. No Win No Fee.

Personal injury solicitors in Ireland almost always work on a no win no fee basis — meaning you pay nothing upfront and nothing if you lose. But understanding what percentage they take, and what costs you might still be responsible for, is important before you sign anything.

What no win no fee actually means

In a no win no fee arrangement, you pay your solicitor nothing unless you receive compensation. If you win, the solicitor deducts their fee — typically around 25% — from your settlement. If you lose, you pay nothing to your own solicitor. However, you should clarify whether you might be liable for the other side's legal costs if you lose — this varies by case.

The PIAB process

Most personal injury claims in Ireland must go through the Personal Injuries Assessment Board (PIAB) before court proceedings can be issued. PIAB assesses the injury and makes an award. If you accept it, the case is over. If you reject it and go to court, you may be penalised on costs if the court award is lower. Your solicitor will advise on whether to accept.

How long personal injury cases take

A claim that settles through PIAB typically takes 9–18 months. A case that goes to court can take 3–5 years. The vast majority of cases settle before a full trial. Your solicitor's job is to maximise your settlement and minimise the time it takes.

What percentage is fair

25% is standard in Ireland. Be wary of any arrangement where the solicitor's fee is unclear or where there are additional charges not explained upfront. Ask for everything in writing before you proceed.

What Happened Next

How Aoife's situation resolved

Outcome

Aoife's solicitor submitted a PIAB application with medical reports and evidence of loss of earnings. PIAB assessed the claim and awarded €28,000. The solicitor advised Aoife to accept. After the solicitor's fee of 25%, Aoife received approximately €21,000. Her total out-of-pocket cost before the settlement was zero.
