Hourly rates in Ireland

Most solicitors in Ireland charge between €200 and €450 per hour depending on experience and location. Dublin rates tend to be higher than regional rates. Always ask for a costs notice before agreeing to proceed — solicitors are legally required to provide one.

Fixed fees vs. hourly billing

For straightforward work like conveyancing, wills, and some family law matters, many solicitors offer fixed fees. For contested matters — disputes, court cases, negotiations — hourly billing is more common. Always clarify upfront which applies to your case.

No win no fee

Personal injury cases in Ireland are almost always handled on a no win no fee basis. The solicitor takes a percentage of your settlement — typically around 25% — only if you win. You pay nothing if the case is unsuccessful. This does not apply to most other areas of law.

Legal aid

If you cannot afford a solicitor, you may qualify for civil legal aid through the Legal Aid Board. Eligibility is means-tested. Criminal legal aid is available in criminal cases where there is a risk of imprisonment. Apply through your nearest Law Centre.
