After a Procedure: Getting Care Back on Track

Radu came to Dublin from Romania for a medical procedure that he believed would improve his health. The operation went ahead as planned, but what happened afterwards changed everything. After being discharged, he was prescribed medication to help with his recovery. Within days, however, Radu developed a severe adverse reaction. His condition deteriorated rapidly, and he found himself back in hospital, seriously unwell. It became clear that the medication prescribed to him after his procedure was incorrect for his medical profile.

Radu faced a painful period of additional treatment and recovery. The mistake meant extra hospitalisation, more medical interventions, and weeks of suffering that should never have happened. He felt let down by a healthcare system he had trusted. Beyond the physical pain, there was the frustration of knowing that proper care after his initial procedure would have prevented this entirely. Radu needed answers and support to move forward.

With legal guidance, Radu's case was built on the simple fact that the wrong medication had been prescribed—something that fell below the standard of care expected. Medical records clearly showed the error. The healthcare provider recognised the mistake, and after careful negotiation, a settlement was reached. Radu received compensation that reflected not only his additional hospitalisation and treatment costs, but also the physical and emotional impact of the error. The settlement allowed him to focus on his recovery without the added burden of financial worry.

What the Law Says

When a healthcare professional prescribes medication, they have a legal duty to do so safely and with proper care. If a patient is harmed because of a medication error—such as prescribing the wrong drug, wrong dose, or a medication unsuitable for that patient's medical history—this may amount to medical negligence. The patient can then pursue a claim for compensation, which covers medical costs, loss of earnings, and damages for pain and suffering caused by the error.

⏰ Important Time Limits

If you believe you have suffered harm due to a medication error or other medical negligence in Ireland, you generally have two years from the date of the injury (or from when you reasonably became aware of it) to start a legal claim. This deadline is strict, and missing it can mean losing your right to compensation entirely. If you are considering a claim, it is important to seek legal advice as soon as possible.
