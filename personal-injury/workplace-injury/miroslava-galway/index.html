How a Care Worker Won Full Compensation After a Lifting Injury

Miroslava worked as a care assistant in a Galway nursing home. A moment without proper support changed everything.

Miroslava had been working as a care assistant for three years. She was dependable, patient with the residents, and genuinely cared about her work. One afternoon while helping a resident out of bed, she was asked to lift without a hoist machine and without a second person to help. Her employer had given her basic training years earlier, but nothing recent. As she lifted, she felt a sharp pain shoot through her lower back. The injury left her unable to work and in constant discomfort.

The pain wasn't just physical. Miroslava became anxious about her future. She worried about money, about whether she'd ever be able to return to the work she loved. She couldn't sleep properly and found herself withdrawn. Her GP referred her to a counsellor. After months of struggling, Miroslava decided to seek legal advice about what had happened.

It became clear that her employer had failed in a basic duty—to keep her safe at work. Manual handling training should have been regular and updated. Equipment should have been available. A second person should have been present. The employer's negligence was straightforward. The legal claim covered her medical treatment, lost wages, and the genuine psychological injury she'd suffered. The full award reflected not just the physical harm, but the real impact on her mental health and quality of life.

What the Law Says

Under Irish Health and Safety law, employers have a duty to protect workers from injury. For care work involving manual handling, this means providing proper equipment (like hoists), ensuring staff are trained and trained regularly, and making sure safe systems are in place—such as having two people lift a patient when needed. If an employer fails to do these things and a worker is injured as a result, the worker can claim compensation. This includes not just physical injury but also any psychological harm caused by the accident or by the injury itself.

Important Time Limit

⏱ Do Not Delay

You have three years from the date of injury to take a legal claim for a workplace injury in Ireland. After three years, you lose the right to claim, with very few exceptions. If you've been injured at work, it's important to get advice early—even if you're still recovering or unsure whether to proceed.
