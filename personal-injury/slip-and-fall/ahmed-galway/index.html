Ahmed Won €28,500 After Slipping on a Wet Supermarket Floor in Galway

No safety mat. No warning sign. Just a wet entrance and a serious injury.

Settlement reached out of court in just over 9 months.

What Happened

It was a Friday afternoon in October when Ahmed popped into his local supermarket in Galway for his weekly shop. The weather had turned wet — heavy rain was coming down outside, the kind that makes you want to hurry indoors as quickly as possible.

As Ahmed stepped through the entrance doors, his foot went out from under him. The floor just inside the door was completely wet, slick from rain brought in by other shoppers. There was no mat, no warning sign, nothing to stop him falling hard onto the concrete floor. He landed on his left shoulder and arm, and the pain was immediate and sharp.

A staff member helped him up, but it was clear something was badly wrong. Ahmed's shoulder was swelling rapidly, and he could barely move his arm. He was taken to the hospital by ambulance where the tests revealed a fractured shoulder and torn rotator cuff muscles. He would need surgery followed by months of physiotherapy.

The injury kept Ahmed off work for nearly six months. He works as a delivery driver — a job that demands two working arms and physical strength. During his recovery, he lost significant income. The medical bills piled up too: hospital treatment, imaging scans, surgery, and ongoing physiotherapy sessions. Even after he returned to work, he wasn't back to full strength or his full earning capacity.

Ahmed decided to pursue a claim. He knew the supermarket had a responsibility to keep their entrance safe, especially during wet weather. He contacted a solicitor through eSolicitors.ie who took on his case and began gathering evidence of his injuries, his lost wages, and the supermarket's negligence.

What the Law Says

In Ireland, the Occupiers Liability Act 1995 is the law that protects people who are injured on someone else's property — whether it's a supermarket, a shop, a restaurant, or any other place open to the public.

This law says that property owners and businesses have a legal duty of care to their visitors. They must take reasonable steps to keep their premises safe and to warn visitors of any dangers. If they fail to do this, and someone gets injured as a result, they can be held liable and have to pay compensation.

In Ahmed's case, the supermarket had failed to:

  • Place a non-slip mat at the entrance during wet weather
  • Put up a warning sign about the wet floor
  • Implement a cleaning or drying routine for the entrance area during rain
  • Take any reasonable precautions to prevent customers slipping

These were all things the supermarket could and should have done. Because they didn't, and because Ahmed was injured as a direct result, the supermarket was liable under Irish occupiers liability law.

What Ahmed Was Entitled To

Ahmed's compensation settlement of €28,500 covered several different elements of his loss and injury. Here's what was included:

  • Pain and suffering: €12,000 for the shoulder injury, the psychological impact of the accident, and ongoing discomfort during recovery
  • Lost earnings: €10,500 for the six months Ahmed was unable to work as a delivery driver
  • Medical expenses: €3,200 for hospital treatment, scans, surgery, and physiotherapy sessions
  • Future care and loss of earnings capacity: €2,800 to account for the fact that Ahmed may not return to his full earning potential immediately

This is a typical settlement for a serious slip and fall injury in Ireland. The amount depends on how severe the injury is, how long recovery takes, how much income is lost, and the specific circumstances of the accident. Settlements can range anywhere from €10,000 for minor injuries to €60,000 or more for severe, long-term injuries.

⚠️ Important: Don't Miss the Time Limit

You have only 2 years from the date of your accident to begin a legal claim. This is the statute of limitations under Irish law. If you slip, trip, or fall in a shop or public place, you must take action within 24 months of the incident or you will lose your right to claim compensation forever.

Don't wait. If you have been injured in a slip or fall accident, contact a solicitor or legal advisor as soon as possible to protect your rights.

What Should You Do Now?

If you've suffered a slip or fall injury in Galway or anywhere else in Ireland, here are the steps you should take:

  1. Seek Medical Attention: Your health is the priority. Get checked by a doctor and keep all medical records and receipts.
  2. Report the Incident: Tell the shop or property owner/manager about the accident immediately and ask them to file an incident report.
  3. Gather Evidence: Take photos of the hazard that caused your fall, the location, and your injuries if possible. Get names and contact details of any witnesses.
  4. Keep Records: Document all expenses related to your injury, including medical bills, lost wages, travel costs, and any other out-of-pocket expenses.
  5. Contact a Solicitor: Reach out to a personal injury solicitor in Galway who specializes in slip and fall claims. Many offer free initial consultations.
  6. Don't Accept Settlement Offers Hastily: Wait until you understand the full extent of your injuries before agreeing to any compensation.
