⚖️ Mortgage Arrears — Your Rights

Can't Pay Your
Mortgage in Ireland.

Falling behind on your mortgage in Ireland is frightening. But you have more rights than you probably think. Lenders cannot simply take your home — there is a process, and there are ways to protect yourself at every stage of it.

Real Story

What happened to Declan

"I lost my job in March. By June I had missed three payments. The bank started sending letters I was too scared to open."
— Declan, Portlaoise, Co. Laois

Declan had been paying his mortgage for eleven years without a problem. When his employer closed, he found himself in arrears within months. Letters from the bank escalated quickly. His wife was terrified they would lose the house. A neighbour told him to get a solicitor before things went further.

The Law

What lenders can and cannot do under Irish law

Under the Central Bank's Code of Conduct on Mortgage Arrears (CCMA), lenders are legally required to follow a structured process before they can begin any legal action against you. This process is called the Mortgage Arrears Resolution Process (MARP).

Before a lender can apply to court to repossess your home, they must engage with you through MARP, consider all alternative repayment arrangements, and wait a minimum period. Repossession proceedings can only begin after this process has been exhausted. A lender who skips these steps can have proceedings struck out by the courts.

Alternative arrangements available under MARP include a temporary interest-only period, a payment moratorium, an extension of the mortgage term, or a split mortgage arrangement. You have the right to an independent appeals process if you are unhappy with a lender's decision.

⚠ Act early — your options narrow over time

In debt and financial matters, the longer you wait the fewer options you have. Getting legal advice early — before a court date, before a judgment is registered, before enforcement begins — gives you significantly more room to negotiate and protect yourself.

What You're Facing

The real consequences

🏠
MARP process must be followed
Your lender cannot go straight to court. They must engage with you through the Mortgage Arrears Resolution Process first — this can take months and gives you time.
⚖️
Repossession is not immediate
Even if court proceedings begin, there are further stages before a repossession order is granted. The process from first missed payment to losing your home typically takes years.
💰
Your credit record
Mortgage arrears are recorded on your credit file through the Central Credit Register. This affects future borrowing. A solicitor can sometimes negotiate how arrears are reported.
🚗
Personal Insolvency
If arrears are severe and unmanageable, a Personal Insolvency Arrangement (PIA) through a Personal Insolvency Practitioner may protect your home while restructuring debt.
📋
Free supports available
MABS (Money Advice and Budgeting Service) provides free independent advice on mortgage arrears. A solicitor is essential once legal proceedings begin.
What Happened Next

How Declan's situation resolved

Outcome

Declan's solicitor wrote to the bank immediately, formally engaging the MARP process on his behalf. The solicitor negotiated a six-month interest-only arrangement while Declan found new work. When Declan's employment resumed, the solicitor helped negotiate a restructured repayment plan that added the arrears to the end of the term. The bank accepted. Declan kept his home.
