Car Repossession
in Ireland. Can They Take It?

If you have fallen behind on car finance payments in Ireland, the finance company may be threatening repossession. But they cannot simply take your car — there are rules they must follow, and in many cases there are options to protect your vehicle or negotiate a resolution.

The Law

What finance companies can and cannot do under Irish law

Under the Consumer Credit Act 1995, a finance company's right to repossess a vehicle depends on how much of the agreement you have paid off. If you have paid less than one-third of the total hire purchase price, the finance company can repossess the vehicle without a court order — but they cannot repossess it from your home without your consent.

If you have paid more than one-third of the total price, the finance company must obtain a court order before repossessing the vehicle. This is known as the 'one-third rule' and is a significant protection for borrowers.

Regardless of how much you have paid, a finance company cannot take the vehicle by force, enter private property without consent, or harass you. Any repossession must comply with the Consumer Protection Code and relevant regulations. Voluntary surrender is always an option but should only be considered after taking legal advice.

⚠ Act early — your options narrow over time

In debt and financial matters, the longer you wait the fewer options you have. Getting legal advice early — before a court date, before a judgment is registered, before enforcement begins — gives you significantly more room to negotiate and protect yourself.

What You're Facing

The real consequences

🏠
The one-third rule
If you have paid more than one-third of the total finance price, the company needs a court order to repossess. Many people don't know this and hand over keys unnecessarily.
⚖️
Cannot take from your home without consent
A repossession agent cannot enter your private property or take your vehicle from your driveway or garage without your permission — regardless of how much you owe.
💰
Shortfall debt
If the car is repossessed and sold for less than you owe, you remain liable for the shortfall. This can result in further legal action against you.
🚗
Voluntary surrender implications
Voluntarily returning the vehicle does not end your obligation — you may still owe money. Get legal advice before surrendering anything.
📋
Negotiation is usually possible
Finance companies often prefer a restructured payment plan to the cost and hassle of repossession. A solicitor can negotiate this before it goes further.
What Happened Next

How Bogdan's situation resolved

Outcome

Bogdan's solicitor calculated that he had paid over 40% of the total hire purchase price — well above the one-third threshold. The finance company had no right to repossess without a court order. The solicitor wrote formally to the finance company, citing the Consumer Credit Act. The repossession was called off. A three-month payment holiday was agreed while Bogdan recovered.
